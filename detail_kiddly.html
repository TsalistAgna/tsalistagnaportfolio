Kiddly

Project Overview

Kiddly is a parental companion application that helps parents understand what their children actually consume during screen time. Rather than only tracking how long children use digital devices, Kiddly analyzes viewing content locally on the device and provides parents with simple, easy-to-understand insights.

The application combines AI-powered content recognition with a privacy-first approach. All analysis runs locally on the device, ensuring that children's viewing activities remain private while still providing meaningful summaries for parents.

As the Product Designer, I designed the user experience—from research and information to high-fidelity interfaces and interactive prototypes. The goal was to make digital parenting less stressful by transforming complex viewing data into actionable insights.

The Problem

Parents Know Screen Time, But Not Screen Content

Most parental control apps focus on limiting screen time but provide little visibility into what children are actually watching. As a result, parents struggle to understand whether screen time is educational, entertaining, or potentially inappropriate.

The challenge was to design an experience that enables parents to monitor content instead of simply measuring usage duration. The application needed to present AI-generated insights in a way that feels trustworthy, easy to understand, and respectful of children's privacy.

Design Process

The design process focused on reducing parental anxiety by transforming complex AI-generated content analysis into a simple, friendly, and actionable dashboard.

1

User Research

Interviewed parents to understand concerns around children's screen habits, digital safety, and limitations of existing parental control applications.

2

User Flow

Designed user flows covering child registration, screen monitoring, content summaries, AI-generated reports, and recommendation pages.

3

Wireframing

Created low-fidelity wireframes to organize dashboards, daily reports, content history, and parental notifications before visual exploration.

4

Visual Design

Designed a playful yet trustworthy interface using soft colors, friendly illustrations, and clear information hierarchy to make complex AI insights approachable for parents.

5

Interactive Prototype

Built high-fidelity interactive prototypes to validate navigation, dashboard interactions, and content monitoring workflows before development.

Key Features

  • AI-powered screen content recognition.
  • Daily content summaries for parents.
  • Educational vs entertainment content breakdown.
  • Age-appropriateness analysis.
  • Privacy-first on-device machine learning.
  • Weekly viewing reports and trends.
  • Personalized recommendations for healthier digital habits.
